\noindent {\bf Q2.}  As seen on the midterm \texttt{(i.e., Section \#2)}, consider
$$ 
f(x) = \log(\exp(x) + \exp(-x)) \ .  $$
Evaluating this function directly can be unstable. This time, let's evaluate its derivatives.\\

\noindent {\bf (a)} The derivative of $f$ is given by $f'(x) = \frac{\exp(x) - \exp(-x)}{\exp(x) + \exp(-x)}$. \\
Is $f'$ reasonably well-conditioned at $x = 1.23$? \\

\vskip2cm

\noindent {\bf (b)} Consider numerically approximating $f'(1.23)$ for a given stepsize $h>0$ via the formula 
$$ \frac{f(1.23 + h) - f(1.23)}{h} .$$
If implemented in standard floating point arithmetic, what value of $h$ would you recommend using to minimize the difference from the true answer $f'(1.23)$?


\vskip 2cm

\noindent {\bf (c)} What values of $a,b,c\in\mathbb{R}$ maximize the order of accuracy for approximating $f'(0)$ below? 
$$ \frac{a f(-h) + bf(0) + cf(2h)}{h} $$
